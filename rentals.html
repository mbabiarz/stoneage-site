Required Documents

In order to rent equipment you’ll need to submit a credit application to establish an account with StoneAge. If you already have an account with StoneAge you can skip this step. You will also need to meet the StoneAge customer insurance requirements.

What equipment can I rent?

We can provide everything a customer needs between the pump and the application. All hoses, fittings and adapters necessary to use StoneAge equipment and tooling on the job are available in our rental facilities.

Our rental fleets are equiped with automated and rotary tools for high pressure waterblasting applications, such as:

  • Pipe Cleaning
  • Exchanger Cleaning
  • Surface Prep
  • Tank and Vessel Cleaning
  • Furnace and Boiler Cleaning

Please note: We do not rent pumps.

What are your rental rates?

Equipment can be rented by day, week or month.

Please contact your closest StoneAge branch to get a quote for the specific equipment and time frame that you require. Our staff will also be happy to review how your rental charges can be applied toward equipment purchase, if desired.

What are your payment terms?

Payment is due upon the completion of the rental period stated in the rental agreement. Long-term rentals will be invoiced after the first month’s rent, and on a 30-day cycle thereafter per the terms of your rental agreement.

Do you accept credit cards?

We gladly accept Visa, MasterCard, American Express and Discover cards.

Will I need to leave a deposit to rent?

No, a deposit is not required to rent equipment.

Do you deliver?

Delivery is available upon request within a 50-mile radius of the local rental facility.

Shipping is available on all rentals within the United States.

Do you offer training?

Training will be provided on all rental equipment at no cost through our local rental offices. Contact your local rental facility for scheduling. If you choose to decline the training, you will be required to sign a waiver before renting.

Can I get support on the job site?

Our expert field technicians can meet with you at the jobsite for training on rental tools and to make sure you have the support and equipment you need to successfully complete your job. Our daily rate for a technician is $995, plus incidentals.

Do I need a special type of vehicle to carry the equipment?

Most of our rental equipment can be carried in standard pickup truck or flatbed, but some may require a trailer for additional length. Our rental staff can discuss specific transport needs based on your rental order. We also offer local delivery and shipping options, if required.

Can I reserve equipment ahead of time?

Yes, you can reserve StoneAge equipment for a scheduled job by contacting your local rental facility.

Can I extend my reservation if I need the equipment longer?

Yes, you can extend your equipment rental by contacting your local rental facility.

Can I purchase my rental equipment?

Yes, our purchase option provides an easy way to reduce overall capital investment when you want to add rented equipment to your fleet. You can receive credit equal to:

  • 90% of the paid rentals for a one-month rental
  • 80% of the paid rentals for a two-month rental
  • 70% of the paid rentals for a three-month rental
  • 60% of the paid rentals for a four-month rental
  • 50% of the paid rentals for rentals of five months or longer

Download the Purchase Option Policy Agreement and return to your local rental facility.

Can I take StoneAge equipment out of the state or country?

Yes. Consult our rental staff about considerations.

Why should I rent from StoneAge?

StoneAge engineers and manufactures all the equipment that we rent – but reliable equipment is only part of the rental equation. We readily share decades of waterblasting experience and back up our rentals with expert technical support to make sure our customers are successful when they use our equipment.

Renting from StoneAge is a great option when you want to:

  • Expand your fleet temporarily during times of peak use
  • Quickly get back-up equipment in the middle of a job
  • Try out newly released stoneage equipment
  • Use rent to own options for greater flexibility
